Mission

The mission of the NYU Center for Social and Political Behavior is to promote scientific research, scholarship, education, and intellectual outreach concerning human behavior in social and political contexts. We sponsor colloquia, seminars, and worskshops and provide a network for collaboration and communication involving faculty and student affiliates in departments throughout the university.

Exemplary Research Topics

  • The structure and functions of attitudes and public opinion.
  • Ideology, religion, and political orientation.
  • Causes and effects of partisanship.
  • Voting intentions and behavior.
  • Perceptions of fairness, legitimacy, and stability of existing (and alternative) social, economic, and political institutions and arrangements.
  • Social media and communication networks.
  • Social stratification and inequality.
  • Stereotyping, prejudice, and discrimination.
  • Motivations for collective action, including voting and protest behavior.
  • Policy preferences concerning global climate change and other current affairs.
